U-shaped Effect of Color in Short-form videos:

Combination of Text, Audio, Image ML and Generative AI

ABSTRACT

Short-form videos are among the fastest-growing forms of online digital sales. While extensive research has focused on image color, the impact of video color on short-form video marketing remains underexplored. In this study, we analyze 2,984 videos containing over 406,420 images and develop a multimethod framework integrating text, audio, and image machine learning techniques (e.g., OpenCV, CNN-BiLSTM, BERT), alongside generative AI tools (e.g., GPT-4, KAN-TTS). Studies 1 and 2 utilize consumption data and apply these machine learning methods to examine the U-shaped relationship between cover-mean and video-peak colors on clicks and sales, respectively. Study 3 investigates how audio arousal and text sentiment moderate these effects. Study 4 uses generative AI to design video stimuli and conducts two randomized online experiments to provide causal evidence, replicating earlier findings. These insights offer a novel understanding of video color strategies and practical guidance for video creators, advertisers, and platform managers in the short-form video industry.

1. Overview Study

This research is structured across 4 studies, as summarized in Figure 1. In Study 1, to examine the first impression of the video, we tested the relationship between video color at the cover-mean level and clicks. In Study 2, to further investigate whether clicks could convert into direct purchase behavior, we tested the U-shaped relationship between video color at the video-peak level and sales. In Study 3, to further validate the impact of the video color, we validate the U-shaped effect of video color and its moderating effect of audio arousal and text sentiment. In Study 4, to provide causal evidence beyond correlations, we first exploit generative AI to design video stimuli, and then launch 2 online experiments. The experiment results for video color consistently replicate the empirical results of previous studies.

2. Data Processing

Figure 2 shows the flowchart for text, audio, and image data processing, which consists of 3 steps. Step 1 is data clean. Three types of unstructured data—text, audio, and image—can be obtained. This involves separating the images (frames) and audio of each recorded video and converting the audio into text via Aliyun's automatic speech recognition (ASR) technology. Step 2 is variables extraction. Various analytical techniques are applied. OpenCV is used to extract color values, CNN-BiLSTM models determine audio arousal levels from the audio, and BERT techniques analyze the text sentiment. Step 3 is empirical analysis. These data are utilized across Studies 1, 2, and 3.

3. Experimental Stimuli in Study 4

In Study 4, two 3 x 2 experiments, Study 4a and Study 4b, were conducted to test the causality effect. We display the stimuli of video color, audio arousal, and text sentiment in the following sections.

3.1 Stimuli Design Leverage Generative AI

We leverage generative AI and follow the procedure shown in Figure 3. Step 1 is text generation with GPT4. We generate texts that merely differ in text sentiment (Study 4b), keeping homogeneity in other text indicators. the text sentiment stimuli used in Study 4b are shown in Table 1. Step 2 is audio generation with Knowledge-Aware Neural TTS (KAN-TTS). Based on the text generated by GPT4, we generate audios that merely differ in arousal (Study 4a), keeping homogeneity in other vocal indicators. Step 3 is image generation with stable video and further to manipulates the video colors. Step 4 is stimulus synthesization. Text, audio, and image obtained from the above 3 steps are synthesized into videos.
